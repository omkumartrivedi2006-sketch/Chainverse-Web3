Foundations

What is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a continuously growing chain of blocks, where each block holds transactions, a timestamp, and the cryptographic hash of the previous block — making the history impossible to rewrite without breaking the whole chain.

Blocks

Containers of transactions grouped together and sealed with a cryptographic hash, then chained to the previous block.

Chain

Every block references its predecessor's hash. Change one byte in old data and every following link goes red.

Decentralized

Thousands of independent nodes keep identical copies. There is no single server to hack or shut down.

Core Pillars

Key Pillars of Web3

Three principles define the new internet — and each one is enforced by math, not promises.

Decentralization

No single company stores your data or decides the rules. The network is owned and operated by its users.

Transparency

Every transaction is verifiable on a public ledger. You can audit, prove, and trust without asking permission.

Security

Cryptography and consensus replace trust. Tampering with one block requires breaking millions of computers.
