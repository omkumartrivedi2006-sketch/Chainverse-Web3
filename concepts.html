From Web2 to Web3

A side-by-side look at how the internet evolves when ownership, identity and money move on-chain.

Web2

Centralized
  • ✕ Companies own your data
  • ✕ Servers can go down or censor
  • ✕ Payments routed through banks
  • ✕ Logins require permissions
VS

Web3

Decentralized
  • ✓ You own your data with a wallet
  • ✓ Thousands of nodes keep it alive
  • ✓ Peer-to-peer payments, global
  • ✓ Sign in with your key, not an email

Ethereum vs Bitcoin

Bitcoin pioneered digital money. Ethereum turned the blockchain into a global computer for any program.

Feature Bitcoin Ethereum
Primary Use Digital money / store of value Smart contracts & dApps
Launched 2009 2015
Block Time ~10 minutes ~12 seconds
Consensus Proof of Work Proof of Stake
Supply Cap 21 million BTC No hard cap (issuance low)
Scripting Limited Turing-complete (Solidity, Vyper)
Token Standard None ERC-20, ERC-721, ERC-1155

Public Key vs Private Key

Your wallet is a pair of cryptographic keys: a public one you share with the world, and a private one you guard for life.

Public Key

Your wallet address. Safe to share — that's how people send you funds.

0x7a3F...bE21

Visible to everyone

Private Key

Your secret signature. Never share it. Whoever owns it owns the wallet.

a91c2f...8dB7 ████

Yours alone

Blockchain vs Traditional Database

Same data, different trust model — one is owned and editable, the other is append-only and verifiable.

Traditional DB

  • ● Central administrator
  • ● Mutable — rows can be edited
  • ● Fast reads & writes
  • ● Single source of truth
  • ● Trust the operator
VS

Blockchain

  • ● No administrator — peer network
  • ● Append-only — history is tamper-evident
  • ● Slower, finality in seconds/minutes
  • ● Thousands of identical copies
  • ● Trust the math & consensus
